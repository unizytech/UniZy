## SUMMARY OF FORMAT STRUCTURE

### Section Order:
1. **Header**: DISCHARGE SUMMARY + Department name
2. **Patient Information**: Left (name, age, address) | Right (REGNO, IPNO, dates, ward, bed)
3. **Medical Team**: Chairman, Unit Head, Admitting Consultant, Unit Consultants, Visiting Consultants
4. **Diagnosis**: Primary diagnosis with full medical terminology
5. **Treatment Summary**: Procedure name and date
6. **History of Present Illness**: Narrative paragraph
7. **Past Medical History**: Comma-separated or narrative
8. **Past Surgical History**: Previous surgeries with years
9. **Physical Examination**: Vitals inline (Temperature, PR, RR, BP, CVS, RS, CNS, P/A)
10. **Investigations**: "Reports Enclosed" or details
11. **Treatment Details**: (New page with patient header)
    - Name of surgery with date
    - Anesthesia
    - Position
    - Finding (bullet points)
    - Operation notes (detailed paragraph)
    - Construction (detailed paragraph)
12. **Course In Hospital**: (New page) Narrative summary
13. **Advice On Discharge**: Numbered list with preamble
14. **Medications**: Numbered list with format: NAME DOSAGE FREQUENCY DURATION
15. **Review Date**: Date in DD.MM.YYYY format
16. **Emergency Care**: (New page with patient header)
    - When to seek care (numbered list)
    - Emergency contact numbers with hours
    - Doctor appointment number
17. **Prepared By**: Doctor name(s)
18. **Signature**: Line with doctor's full credentials

### Key Formatting Notes:
- Patient header repeated on all new pages
- Vitals in one line with bold labels (PR, RR, BP, CVS, RS, CNS, P/A)
- Medications: "TAB. NAME DOSAGE FREQUENCY DURATION"
- Dates: DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM or DD.MM.YYYY
- Bullet points for findings
- Numbered lists for advice and emergency care
- Medical abbreviations preserved: H/o, Dr., Prof., MS, FMAS, etc.
